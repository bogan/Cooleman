About the Group

The Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group is a ParkCare group that "cares" for the Cooleman Ridge Nature Reserve.

Mission

The mission of the Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group is to "restore the bush" by returning the ecology of the nature reserve to it's pre-European state.

History

The group was formed in 1991 by three ladies - Bess Sledge, Pauline Lynga and Tina Nevins.

The group has now grown to have 50 registered members that contribute in various ways with small but active core of members that regulary meet and work on the nature reserve.

Meetings

The group meets once a month on the third Sunday of every month for a group working party. The group works on one of designated group work areas.

Please see our latest newsletter for the specific details for our next meeting.

There are also smaller weekly work groups that focus on just one specific area of the nature reserve. These include:

  • Kathner Street Group - every Friday morning at the Kathner Street Dam site
  • Arawang Group - every Wednesday morning at the Mt Arawang site

Work

The group performs a wide variety of work including:

  • Weed
  • Plant
  • Study the wildlife
  • Take part in theme walks
  • Tree survey
  • Bird monitoring
  • Fire fuel reduction
  • Herbarium compilation
  • Vegetation surveys
  • Waterwatch
  • Photographic record

Partners

The group works with a number of other organisations: These include:

  • Green Army
  • ACT Government
  • Southern ACT Catchement Group
