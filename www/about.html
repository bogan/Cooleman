About

The Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group is a is a volunteer group that "cares" for the Cooleman Ridge Nature Reserve.

Mission

The mission of the Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group that seeks to "regenerate the bush" by returning the ecology of the nature reserve to its pre-European state. Please see our Strategy page for more details about how the group is working to achieve this goal.

The Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group is part of a larger network of Park Care groups based at other reserves throughout the ACT, working with a similar goal to regenerate their own reserve.

Composition

The Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group is made up of various volunteers from a variety of backgrounds, such as scientists, public servants, homekeepers, health professionals, etc.

The group is also comprised of many informal members that do not regularly attend group activities but contribute to the groups work by doing private work while performing some other activity (eg.. walking the dog)

History

The group was formed in 1991 by three ladies - Bess Sledge, Pauline Lynga and Tina Nevins - who were passionate about the ridge and regenerating the ridge. They worked hard on planting, weeding, letter writing, fundraising, recruiting, documenting, collecting, surveying, etc.

These ladies have now been replaced by a new generation of volunteers who carry on their hard work and vision. The group has now grown to have 50 registered members that contribute in various ways to the groups objectives. The group has a small but active core of members that regulary meet and work on the nature reserve.

A more detailed history of the group can be obtained by reading some of the group's newsletters which provide a more detailed account of the actvities of the group over the years.

Achievements

The group has undertakes various activities over they years in support of its goal. the group now claim to have made signficant improvements to the ecology of the ridge. The group has managed to make some major achievements inclue:

  • Reduced or eleminated many weeds.
  • Planted thousands of plants.
  • Surveyed the ridges plants, animals, trees, dams, fires and blackberries.
  • Developed various nature trails.
  • Documented the history.
  • Petitioned for better infrastructure (eg. signage, fencing, roads, foot bridges, etc).
