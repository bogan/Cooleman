About

The Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group is a Park Care group that "cares" for the Cooleman Ridge Nature Reserve.

The Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group is part of a larger network of other parkcare groups working throughout the ACT. See our Partners page for more details.

Mission

The mission of the Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group is to "regenerate the bush" by returning the ecology of the nature reserve to its pre-European state. Please see our Strategy page for more details about how the group is working to achieve this goal.

Membership

The Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group is small but active group made up of various volunteers from a variety of backgrounds, such as scientists, public servants, homekeepers.

The currrent membership is approximately 50 registered members. However, the active core of the group is approximately a dozen or so.

Membership is free an open to anybody. If you'd like to become a member of the Cooleman Ridge Park Care Group or join up, please contact us via our Contact page.

The group is also comprised of many informal members that do not regularly attend group activities but contribute to the groups work by doing private work while performing some other activity (eg.. walking the dog)

History

The group was formed in 1991 by three ladies - Bess Sledge, Pauline Lynga and Tina Nevins - and now grown to have 50 registered members that contribute in various ways to the groups objectives.

A more detailed history can be obtained by reading some of the groups newsletters which provides a more detailed account of the actvities of the group over the years.

Achievements

The group has made significant progress with rehabilitating the reserve but much work remains to be done.

The group has managed to make some major achievements.

the achievements are not always obvious.

The major achievments of the group include:

  • Significantly reduced the presence of weeds in the many of the key areas on the ridge.
  • Surveyed many of the significant aspects of the ridge including plants, animals, trees, dams, fires and blackberries
  • Created formal Nature Trails with accompaning brouchures.

You can read more about the important work that group performs in the Our Work section of the web site.

The Cooleman Ridge Nature Reserve is one of the more damaged but there still remain many good areas. These areas serve as the nucleus of the groups efforts to restore the ecology of the ridge.
