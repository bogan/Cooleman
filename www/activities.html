Activities

The group performs a wide variety of activities. These activities are design to support our broad strategy to regenerate the bush. The main activities that the group undertakes are the following:

Weeding

Weeding is the main day to day activity of the group. The main reasonAlthough weeding is not glamourous, the group believes that it is the single most effective activity that can be used to restore the ecology of the ridge.

Our belief in the effectiveness of weeding is based on our own experience with weeding and the experience of similar landcare groups. Please see our Strategy page for a more detailed explanation of why weeding is a vital part of our strategy to regenerate the native vegetation of the ridge.

Planting

Planting has been an important part of the work that the group has performed in the past. Planting is still sometimes performed by the group. The plantings are of various species that are native to the ridge such as native grasses, forbs, shrubs and trees. These plants are either sourceed from seedlings grown off-site or by transplanting small plants from one part of the ridge to another.

Plantings are also performed on the ridge either by other groups (eg. the Green Army) in the planting of new trees, shrubs and grasses. The nature reserve now has some significant plantations. Please visit our Plantations page for more details.

Many of the plants are now very mature and provide many additional benefits for the ridge such as weed suppresion, windbreaks, and habitati for the animals on the ridge.

Surveys

Surveys are often performed by the group. A survey aims to record a significant aspect of the nature reserve (eg. plants, animals, weeds, etc). These surveys provide many benefits such assisting the ongoing management of the ridge, recording the history of the ridge and the groups work, and just pure enjoyment. Please visit one of our various survey pages on Plants, Animals, Blackberrys, Dams, Fires more detailed information about each of the surveys the group performs.

Survey work is an important way to contribute to the group for those who do not wish to perform other important activities such as weeding.

Guided Walks

Guided walks are sometimes organised by the group. These walks have various themes (eg. birds, fire, lichen, weeds, etc). These are conducted by subject matter experts. These guided walks are open to the general public and are generally well attended. These guided walks have been imporant means of communicating the work the group does and of building better relationships with the broader community.

Herbarium

The group maintains a signficant herbarium containing specimums of plants found on the ridge. Specimens are shared with the scientific community (e.g. National Botanical Gardens).

Photography

The group maintains a collection of photographs that relate to the ridge. These photos are old and new and cover all aspects of the ridge from animals and birds to the people and work. The photos are used to help monitor the changes in the ridge as well as providing content for the web site.
